Database Fundamentals

What Is a Database? ▼

A database is a structured collection of data with internal links that make information accessible from many perspectives. This is different from a traditional flat file, which stores information in only one single view.

Why Database Systems Are Important ▼

  • Integrate data across departments (payroll, inventory, personnel, sales).
  • Reduce duplication and inconsistency.
  • Allow organizations to analyze large amounts of information efficiently.
  • Support decision-making through data mining and reporting.

Real-World Impact ▼

Modern websites like Google, Amazon, and eBay rely heavily on database systems to deliver fast, personalized, and relevant information to users.
